# Business Model

## Principle

Vestra should earn money by helping users make better wardrobe decisions, not by pressuring them to buy more.

The free product must be genuinely useful. Premium should unlock deeper intelligence, planning, convenience, and visual confidence.

## Free Plan

Purpose: help users understand Vestra's value and build their wardrobe graph.

Possible free features:

- account creation;
- limited wardrobe item uploads;
- basic background removal;
- basic AI clothing analysis;
- wardrobe gallery, search, and filters;
- limited AI outfit generations per month;
- saved outfits with basic history;
- language support for Azerbaijani, English, and Russian.

Free plan constraints should be generous enough for trust, but limited enough that active users naturally understand Premium.

## Premium Plan

Purpose: turn Vestra into a daily personal stylist and wardrobe optimization system.

Possible premium features:

- higher or unlimited wardrobe uploads;
- higher AI analysis and stylist generation limits;
- advanced wardrobe insights;
- cost-per-wear tracking;
- laundry and wear history;
- outfit calendar;
- weather-aware daily outfit suggestions;
- trip packing;
- advanced outfit regeneration controls;
- premium virtual try-on;
- mirror selfie mode;
- wardrobe gap analysis;
- priority processing;
- premium support.

Premium should be positioned around saving time, reducing wasted purchases, and dressing better every day.

## Affiliate Model

Affiliate revenue should be compatibility-first.

Vestra can recommend items only when they solve a real wardrobe gap or unlock meaningful outfit combinations.

Rules:

- disclose affiliate relationships clearly;
- never mix owned clothing with recommended items without labeling;
- explain why the recommended item fits the user's wardrobe;
- prefer fewer, higher-confidence recommendations;
- track conversion without compromising user privacy.

Potential affiliate categories:

- wardrobe essentials;
- shoes;
- outerwear;
- accessories;
- care products;
- repair/tailoring services;
- second-hand listings.

## Blogger Program

Fashion bloggers and creators can help Vestra grow through trust and local style context.

Program ideas:

- referral links;
- creator landing pages;
- curated style challenges;
- "build outfits from your own wardrobe" campaigns;
- commission on Premium subscriptions;
- commission on compatible partner purchases;
- creator collections that users can adapt to owned clothing.

Guardrails:

- creators should not force a shopping-first experience;
- sponsored recommendations must be labeled;
- Vestra should preserve user wardrobe compatibility as the ranking priority.

## Local Stores

Local stores can become a differentiated supply layer, especially in Azerbaijan and nearby markets.

Partnership options:

- inventory feeds;
- featured local brand pages;
- compatibility-based recommendations;
- seasonal capsules;
- styling campaigns;
- store pickup integrations;
- local promotions for Premium users.

Value for stores:

- higher-intent traffic;
- better matching between item and buyer;
- lower return risk because recommendations are wardrobe-aware;
- local brand discovery.

## Second-Hand Partnerships

Second-hand marketplaces align strongly with Vestra's sustainability and money-saving vision.

Partnership options:

- second-hand inventory recommendations;
- saved searches for wardrobe gaps;
- resale suggestions for underused items;
- circular wardrobe insights;
- repair, donation, or resale workflows.

Recommendation rules:

- show compatibility with owned wardrobe;
- prioritize condition and quality;
- avoid flooding users with low-signal listings;
- disclose partner relationship;
- help users buy fewer, better items.

## Revenue Streams

Primary:

- monthly Premium subscription;
- annual Premium subscription.

Secondary:

- affiliate commission;
- local store partnerships;
- second-hand marketplace partnerships;
- blogger referral program;
- future B2B styling or inventory compatibility tools.

## Metrics

Core business metrics:

- free-to-premium conversion;
- monthly active users;
- wardrobe upload activation rate;
- AI stylist generation rate;
- saved outfit rate;
- Premium retention;
- churn reasons;
- affiliate conversion rate;
- partner revenue.

Trust metrics:

- outfits using only owned items;
- hallucination prevention success;
- user correction rate;
- avoided duplicate purchases;
- underused items worn again.
